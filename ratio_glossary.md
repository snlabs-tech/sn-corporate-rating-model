## Common Ratio Groups

This section groups a few frequently used ratios by analytical theme (leverage/coverage, profitability/returns, liquidity/maturity).

---

### 1. Leverage / Coverage

#### Debt / EBITDA

- **Expansion**  
  - Debt: Total interest-bearing debt  
  - EBITDA: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation and Amortization  

- **Formula**  
  Debt / EBITDA  

- **What it signifies**  
  Core leverage ratio showing how many years of current operating earnings would be needed to repay total debt. Lower values mean lower financial leverage.

---

#### EBITDA / Interest

- **Expansion**  
  - EBITDA: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation and Amortization  
  - Interest: Interest expense  

- **Formula**  
  EBITDA / Interest expense  

- **What it signifies**  
  Interest coverage ratio based on a cash-flow-like earnings measure. Higher values indicate more comfortable headroom to service interest obligations.

---

#### CFO / Interest

- **Expansion**  
  - CFO: Cash Flow from Operations  
  - Interest: Interest expense  

- **Formula**  
  CFO / Interest expense  

- **What it signifies**  
  Cash-based coverage ratio showing how many times actual operating cash flow covers interest payments. More robust than pure earnings measures when accruals are large.

---

#### CFO / Debt

- **Expansion**  
  - CFO: Cash Flow from Operations  
  - Debt: Total interest-bearing debt  

- **Formula**  
  CFO / Debt  

- **What it signifies**  
  Cash-flow-based leverage metric; indicates how quickly the company could repay debt using current operating cash flows. Higher ratios suggest stronger deleveraging capacity.

---

#### Debt / Capital

- **Expansion**  
  - Debt: Total interest-bearing debt  
  - Capital: Debt + Equity  

- **Formula**  
  Debt / (Debt + Equity)  

- **What it signifies**  
  Capital structure measure showing the share of total capital funded by debt. Higher values mean a more leveraged balance sheet.

---

### 2. Profitability / Returns

#### EBIT Margin

- **Expansion**  
  - EBIT: Earnings Before Interest and Taxes  
  - Revenue: Sales  

- **Formula**  
  EBIT / Revenue  

- **What it signifies**  
  Operating profit margin after depreciation and amortization, as a percentage of revenue. Shows how profitable the company’s day to day operations are.
---

#### EBITDA Margin

- **Expansion**  
  - EBITDA: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation and Amortization  
  - Revenue: Sales  

- **Formula**  
  EBITDA / Revenue  

- **What it signifies**  
  Profit margin before non-cash charges. Higher margins suggest stronger pricing power, cost efficiency, or favourable business mix.

---

#### ROE

- **Expansion**  
  - ROE: Return on Equity  

- **Formula**  
  Net income / Shareholders’ equity  

- **What it signifies**  
  Profitability from the perspective of equity investors. Higher ROE is attractive, but should be assessed together with leverage.

---

#### ROA

- **Expansion**  
  - ROA: Return on Assets  

- **Formula**  
  Net income / Total assets  

- **What it signifies**  
  Measures how efficiently the company uses its assets to generate profit. Higher ROA indicates better asset productivity.

---

### 3. Liquidity / Maturity

#### Current Ratio

- **Expansion**  
  - Current assets: Assets expected to be converted to cash within 12 months  
  - Current liabilities: Obligations due within 12 months  

- **Formula**  
  Current assets / Current liabilities  

- **What it signifies**  
  Basic measure of short-term liquidity. Ratios well above 1.0 indicate that short-term assets exceed short-term liabilities.

---

#### Cash / Short‑Term Debt

- **Expansion**  
  - Cash: Cash and cash equivalents  
  - Short-term debt: Debt maturing within 12 months  

- **Formula**  
  Cash / Short-term debt  

- **What it signifies**  
  Tests how much of near-term maturing debt could be covered immediately by on-balance-sheet cash. Values ≥1.0 indicate that cash alone could repay all short-term debt.

---

#### Cash / Total Debt

- **Expansion**  
  - Cash: Cash and cash equivalents  
  - Total debt: All interest-bearing debt  

- **Formula**  
  Cash / Total debt  

- **What it signifies**  
  Indicates the extent to which total debt is backed by immediately available cash. Higher values mean a stronger liquidity buffer relative to overall leverage.

---

#### “Maturity Wall” Ratio

- **Expansion**  
  - Debt maturing in near term: Debt falling due in a defined window (e.g. next 12–24 months)  
  - Total debt: All interest-bearing debt  

- **Formula**  
  Debt maturing in a near-term window / Total debt  

- **What it signifies**  
  Shows concentration of maturities in the short-to-medium term (“maturity wall”). High values indicate significant refinancing risk in the chosen window and may require strong liquidity or market access to manage.
